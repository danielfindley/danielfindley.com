Methodology & How to Read This

Each curve shows the probability distribution of FPL points a player could score in the upcoming gameweek, estimated from 10,000 Monte Carlo simulations.

How the simulations work: For each simulation we sample goals (Poisson), assists (Poisson), clean sheets (Bernoulli), bonus points (correlated with goals/assists/CS via a trained model), cards (Bernoulli), and goals-conceded penalties. These are converted to FPL points using official scoring rules and summed to produce a total for that trial. The resulting 10,000 totals are smoothed into the KDE curves you see below.

How to read the plot:

  • The width/spread of a curve shows outcome variance — wider means less predictable.
  • The red dashed line marks the expected value E[pts].
  • Hover over any curve to see detailed stats and the probability of scoring ≥ any point threshold.
  • Use the filters to isolate positions and the sort dropdown to reorder by different metrics.

Model inputs include rolling xG, xGA, fixture difficulty, home/away, and historical per-90 stats. All predictions are for a single gameweek.
